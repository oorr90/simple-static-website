Isle Royale National Park

An island in Lake Superior

Pure Michigan

Explore a rugged, isolated island, far from the sights and sounds of civilization. Surrounded by Lake Superior, Isle Royale offers unparalleled solitude and adventures for backpackers, hikers, boaters, kayakers, canoeists and scuba divers. Here, amid stunning scenic beauty, you'll find opportunities for reflection and discovery, and make memories that last a lifetime.

Planning a Trip?

Each year the park publishes a newspaper, the The Greenstone, which provides trip planning information (ferry schedules & rates, camping guidelines, fishing regulations, etc.) in one place for anyone considering a visit to Isle Royale.

Accomodations

There are two rustic, one-room camper cabins that provide a unique alternative to tent camping in Windigo. Amenities include: Table and chairs, futon sofa, two bunk beds with mattresses, electrical outlets, and an outdoor picnic table and grill.

Wildlife

A number of habitats exist on the island, the primary being boreal forest, similar to neighboring Ontario and Minnesota. Upland areas along some of the ridges are effectively "balds" with exposed bedrock and a few scrubby trees, blueberry bushes, and hardy grasses.

Getting Here

The island is accessible by private boat, seaplane, National Park Service ferry service from Houghton, Michigan (the Ranger III), ferry service from Copper Harbor, Michigan (Isle Royale Queen IV), and ferry service from Grand Portage, Minnesota (Voyageur II and Sea Hunter).

Wolves & Moose

The single predator-single prey relationship between wolves and moose on Isle Royale in Lake Superior is unique, and has been the subject of detailed study for over 55 years. Over the fifty years of the study, the populations of both moose and wolves have shown repeated spikes and declines and have not settled to a balanced relationship.
